# Punch and Grog

## Punch and Grog

The principal components of Punch and Grog are Jamaican rum (70–74%) and Goa or Batavia arrack (55–58%), refined sugar of first quality (free from bluish tint), as well as citric acid and aromatic spirits (vanilla, rose, etc.), and fresh tinctures (or alcoholates) of lemon peel, orange peel, and so on.

For lower-grade quality, good imitations of rum or arrack may be used, preferably with the addition of genuine products.

The syrup to be employed should have a high consistency—that is, it should contain at least 1.150 kg of sugar per liter of syrup. To prevent crystallization, 2% by weight of citric acid (per kilogram of sugar used) should be added during cooking.

The sugar must be cooked such that 100 kg of sugar and 45 liters of water yield exactly 88 liters of syrup; it is therefore necessary to heat the mass until it is reduced to the indicated volume.

## Punch Extracts

We shall preface the instructions with some of the principal preparations employed in the composition of various Punch Extracts or Syrups:

**Spirit of Lemon:** The peel of 30 lemons is macerated for 8–10 days in 5 liters of arrack. The alcoholate is then strained and filtered.

**Spirit of Orange:** The white pith is removed from the fresh peel of 20 oranges, which is then macerated for 15 days in 2 liters of rum.

**Spirit of Vanilla:** 50 grams of chopped vanilla is macerated for 15 days in 2 liters of arrack.

**Spirit of Rose:** A solution of 1 gram of essential oil of rose in 9 grams of alcohol of 90°.

We now indicate several recipes for Punch Extracts, the range of which may vary infinitely, with the note that, in general, one liter of extract is sufficient to prepare 3–4 liters of punch, imparting a sweet and aromatic flavor.
