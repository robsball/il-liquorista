## Blue (Indigo Dye)

### Overview
The most commonly used is indigo (see p. 140); it is prepared with a solution:

### Method I: Direct Dissolution

**Ingredients:**
- Indigo, dried and in fine powder: 50 gr
- Concentrated pure sulfuric acid: 400 gr

**Procedure:**
1. Mix in a porcelain capsule and leave the mixture for 5-6 days, stirring frequently with a glass rod.
2. Slowly pour this solution into two liters of distilled water (NOT in reverse!) in a wide porcelain or earthenware vessel and leave again for one day.
3. Finally, neutralize by carefully and gradually adding 150 (or 300) grams of calcium carbonate to the mixture: once effervescence ceases, leave to rest.
4. Decant the liquid, add 240 gr of alcohol (90°), and finally filter through paper.

### Alternative: Commercial Indigo Extract

It is possible to use indigo extract or indigo paste found in commerce to prepare the tincture. Mix:
- Indigo extract: 100 gr
- Alcohol 90°: 200 gr
- Distilled water: 600 gr

Leave the mixture undisturbed until dissolved, then filter through paper.

### Method II: Direct Use of Indigo Carmine at 5%

When it is desired to use the tincture directly at 5% indigo carmine, disperse the fine powder indigo carmine 5 in distilled water 80, then add alcohol (90°) 25: subsequently filter.

### Method III: Extended Process (Improved Product)

A longer procedure, which yields a better product, consists of treating indigo with sulfuric acid as described above, then pouring the liquid while continuously stirring into 8 liters of distilled water. In this water, immerse pieces of flannel or scraps of wool and bring to a boil: the fabric appropriates the coloring matter. Then:

1. Wash thoroughly with water until acidity is eliminated.
2. Boil with 6 liters of water alkalinized with 5 gr of potassium carbonate and wash the fabric well; it will surrender the coloring substance to the water.
3. Filter through paper, add 700 gr of alcohol (90°), and filter again.
