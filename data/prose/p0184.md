For very strongly sweetened liqueurs, besides the sugar syrup, a large quantity of water is required to dilute alcohol of 95°. It is advisable to first dilute the alcohol with most of the water that will be needed, because strong alcohol has the property of drawing water away from the syrup, which causes the sugar to separate out in fine crystals. With weaker alcohol there is no need to fear this problem. The remaining water is used to rinse out the vessel in which the sugar was boiled.

In the case of strongly sugared liqueurs — where, even if they must be prepared with 95° alcohol, the refined sugar syrup is almost sufficient by itself to dilute the alcohol — it is necessary to calculate what quantity of water should be used to obtain the syrup.
