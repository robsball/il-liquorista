# Considerations and Criteria for Preparing Aromatized Spirits and Tinctures (Extracts) by Distillation or by Digestion

**Observations.** — As with maceration, the volume of aromatized alcohol obtained from the apparatus after digestion — especially when carried out in the alembic or in a simple vessel that can be sealed tightly, so as to leave it immersed in warm water (40-50°C) for 2-3 days — always turns out to be less than the amount of alcohol originally used to exhaust the drugs, since these always retain a portion of it. Therefore, in order to reach the prescribed volume of aromatized alcohol, after drawing off the cold liquid and pressing out the plant material, the residue is treated with the amount of water needed to complete the indicated volume of aromatized spirit. This water is poured over the exhausted substances, stirred with an agitator, and the material is pressed again after one day of contact.

The aromatized spirits obtained either by digestion or by maceration must, after being collected, be promptly filtered.

**Considerations and Criteria for Preparing Aromatized Spirits and Tinctures (Extracts) by Distillation or by Digestion**

Through digestion, alcohol dissolves both the volatile principles (essential oils) and the fixed extractive substances (bitter substances, alkaloids, and other substances dissolved in the plant juice); with distillation, on the other hand, only the volatile essential oils pass into the alcohol, while the extractive matter remains in the still residue.

If, therefore, a drug contains valuable extractive substances — whether alone or combined with essential oils — or is so rich in essential oils that the extractive substances (even if harmful to flavor) disappear completely, the tincture must be prepared by maceration or digestion. All tinctures of herbs, roots, barks, peels, etc. listed in the index must therefore be prepared using these methods.

If, instead, the drugs contain only one usable substance — the essential oil — together with a large quantity of extractive matter that is worthless or harmful to flavor, the substances must be prepared by distillation (spirits/"alcoolati").
