# Processes

### Distillation

Many liquid bodies (as well as many solid ones) are transformed by the action of heat into the vapor state, from which state, following cooling, they can once again be reduced to the liquid state.

If the process of evaporation of a liquid takes place in the open, in free air, we call it evaporation.

If the evaporation takes place in apparatus built for the purpose, equipped with condensers for condensing the vapors, the process is called distillation, and the new liquid thus obtained is called the distillate or product of distillation.

The purpose of distillation, then, is to separate by means of heat the more volatile parts of a substance, transforming them into vapor, the condensation of which is then brought about by cooling, so as to collect it afterward as a liquid. This operation is delicate and requires particular care and skill.

The purpose of distillation for the alcohol manufacturer is to separate the alcoholic part of any liquid that has previously undergone vinous or alcoholic fermentation.

The purpose of the liqueur-maker who distills, however, is almost always to extract, whether by means of water or of alcohol, the aromas from aromatic substances — that is to say, his aim is to flavor liquids.

Distillation is carried out in apparatus called, using a word of Arabic origin, stills (*alambicchi*), known since very ancient times. Their shape varies greatly from country to country, according to the substance that must be treated in them and the purpose to be achieved. Heating can be applied in various ways, namely: direct fire, water bath, sand bath, steam, etc.
