We shall close this chapter on the preparation of alcoholates with some standard guidelines adopted by the principal producers and distillers of France:

1) In the distillation of any alcoholic liquor, proceed with great slowness, in order not to accumulate an excess of alcoholic vapor, taking care to regulate the heat properly. Proceeding thus, no empyreumatic residues will be obtained.

2) A small proportional quantity of orange-flower water (3–4%) imparts a good fragrance and facilitates the union of the other aromas.

3) If a mixture contains cloves or nutmeg, it is more convenient to incorporate a small quantity of chalk or magnesium carbonate to prevent these aromas from passing over too rapidly.

4) The aromas of orange or citron pass slowly.

5) In a mixture of aromas, mint passes first; hyssop, calamus, and cardamom pass later.
