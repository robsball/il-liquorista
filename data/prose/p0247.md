## Concentrated Essential Oils, Soluble Without Turbidity in 30% Alcohol

Spirits prepared using ethereal oils are generally somewhat opaque or milky, or must be subjected to a clarification process that requires considerable time.

In recent years, extra-strong essential oils have come onto the market, soluble in 30% alcohol, which are completely free of substances that cause turbidity, so that liqueurs made with them result in clarity as soon as they are prepared, and consequently are immediately usable.

The preparation of concentrated ethereal oils is carried out as follows:

For example, if one wishes to prepare concentrated cumin essence, 20 kg of cumin essential oil is mixed with 25 liters of 90% alcohol, the mixture is introduced into a still, 25–30 liters of water are added, it is mixed well, the apparatus is closed, and it is distilled slowly using indirect steam, collecting the distilled product into a demijohn with a capacity of approximately 15 liters.

When this is filled to three-quarters full, it is withdrawn and replaced with another of the same capacity.
