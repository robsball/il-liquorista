# Genuine Cognac

## Genuine Cognac

Various types of brandy are known commercially under this name, each with special denominations according to their respective regions of production.

1. **Cognac Fine-Champagne.** — These are the most highly prized. They are distilled especially in France (Charente); the center of production is Segonzac.

2. **Petit-Champagne.** — The center of this region is Châteauneuf.

3. **Borderies or Premier Bois.** — The principal centers of this quality are Cognac, Hiersac, Farnac, Matha, Angoulême, Barbezieux, Jonzac, Pons, Saintes.

4. **Deuxième Bois or Bon Bois.** — Manufactured at Rouillé and Saint-Jean d'Angély.

5. **Saintonge and La Rochelle.** — These are inferior qualities because they have a certain earthy flavor due to being obtained from vines in damp and salt-laden soils in the vicinity of the sea.

6. **Franzbranntwein** (French grape spirit) **and Armagnac** are superior varieties of cognac.

The distillate of fermented grape must fermentation carries in France the name of Soutirage.

In Italy (Veneto, Piedmont, Sicily) very fine types of cognac are now manufactured, which find markets even abroad (Africa, Brazil).
