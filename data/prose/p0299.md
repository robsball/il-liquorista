The liquid is decanted and the residue is then treated with this mixture:

- Water: 200 g
- Alcohol of 95°: 400 g

Shake vigorously and allow to macerate for another 4 days; decant the liquid and combine it with that obtained previously, then filter through paper. The yellow tint thus obtained is barely different from that of saffron; however, it has the property of turning red upon addition of calcium salts. Since these salts are contained in a certain proportion in spring water, well water, etc., it may happen that a liqueur prepared with such waters remains colored in pale yellow-red when one wishes to color it in clear yellow.

**N.B.** — Turmeric added directly to liqueurs weak in alcohol (45–50°) gives poor coloration; it is therefore preferable to use its tincture.

## Color for Curaçao

- Fernambuco wood: 100 g
- Brazil wood: 100 g
- Cream of tartar: 4 g
- Alcohol of 90°: 0.5 l

Arrange the woods in vessels of glazed earthenware in distinct layers, dust them with cream of tartar, then pour the alcohol over them, allowing them to macerate for approximately 8 days. Finally, completely exhaust the residue with a fresh dose of alcohol.
