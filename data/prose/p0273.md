# Sugar Syrups: Preparation and Composition

Under such conditions, the syrup contains 100 parts water and 190 parts sugar.

Sugar syrups are prepared hot with:

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Sugar | 1.750 kg |
| Water | 1 L |

Heat to boiling, strain, then filter the liquid.

It is also possible to prepare the syrup cold, using approximately 5% more sugar: sugar 1.800 kg, water 1 kg.

However, the proportions of water and sugar indicated above for syrup preparation are not absolute; indeed, one must not forget that they should be adjusted according to the nature and composition of the liquid one wishes to transform into syrup, or the quality of the liqueur to be obtained, as well as the taste of consumers. In this regard, it is well to remember the limits of sugar percentage that are permitted for different classes of liqueurs:

| Liqueur Type | Sugar Content (per liter) |
|---|---|
| Rosoli or Creams | 350–450 g |
| Fine Liqueurs | 175–325 g |
| Ordinary Liqueurs | 50–115 g |
| Aqua vitae | 40–85 g |

The syrup in the proportions indicated above, which correspond to the use of 57 liters of water per quintal of sugar, is very concentrated; however, it is advisable to prepare it under such conditions, because in this way it can be kept unaltered in barrels, etc.; when needed, it is diluted with water to the desired strength.

Hot preparation is always to be preferred; the addition of 100–200 g of citric acid per quintal of sugar is also recommended, in order to prevent crystallization, which occurs frequently, especially in concentrated syrups.

The syrup prepared in the proportions indicated above, namely 1.750 kg sugar per liter of water, or better, 57 liters of water per quintal of sugar, weighs 157 kg, so the sugar percentage comes to about 93–94%.
