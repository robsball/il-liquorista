**Herb Bennet (Cariofillata).** — This plant, also called Blessed Herb, wild avens, or clove-root, is the common *Geum urbanum*, found in hedges, moist soils, etc.

The root, as thick as a quill, with many adventitious rootlets, is brownish-yellow at the margins and violet at the center. Fresh, it has a slight clove-like odor and a sharp, astringent taste. It contains a bitter principle (Geine) and an essential oil.

**Caraway (Carvi).** — This is the Kümmel of the Germans, or meadow cumin. *Carum carvi* grows wild throughout Europe and is very common in our meadows. However, it is cultivated on a large scale in Russia, Holland, Germany, England, etc. The seeds are black, oblong, and pointed, about 5 mm long; they have a strong, aromatic taste. They contain 3 to 7% essential oil. The finest come from the more northern regions.

**Cascarilla.** — The bark of *Croton eluteria* (Euphorbiaceae) comes from the Antilles and the Balearic Islands. It has an acrid, bitter taste and a strong aromatic odor, which becomes even more intense when burned. In appearance it closely resembles grey cinchona bark, but can be distinguished from it by smell and taste. It contains 1 to 3% essential oil.

**Citrons (Cedri).** — These are the fruits of *Citrus medica*, *C. macrocarpa*, and other varieties.

They vary in size from that of a lemon (the Florence citron) up to 70 cm in circumference (the "monstrous" citron). In Italy they are produced especially in Calabria, along the shores of Lake Garda, etc.

From citron peels an essential oil is extracted — very fluid, slightly yellowish, with a distinctive odor and aromatic taste.

**Cedar (wood).** — The wood of *Juniperus virginiana*, or American red cedar, is cinnamon-colored or reddish, with an aromatic odor. By distillation it yields a dense essential oil, colorless or reddish, with a characteristic odor. It is soluble in alcohol.
