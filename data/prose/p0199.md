# Weights and Measures

The raw materials which, either directly or after being transformed into suitable preparations, become part of the composition of liqueurs must be used in those specific proportions capable of imparting the flavor and the particular characteristics of density and appearance that serve to distinguish the various liqueurs. Such proportions are either indicated in the numerous recipes established or sanctioned by practice, or else are studied, modified, or even established through the initiative and skill of the manufacturer.

It is therefore evident that a necessary condition is the accurate and rigorous dosing of the various substances, and it is consequently indispensable for the manufacturer to have sound practical skill in the handling of weighing and measuring, founded on complete knowledge of the weights and measures currently in use — not only these, but also some of the older ones, as well as those adopted in other countries. This is because it is sometimes necessary, when consulting old or foreign formularies or treatises, to find weights and measures quite different from those of the current decimal metric system, and one must know their different values and their conversion to it. Likewise, in commercial dealings it is quite common to receive imported products valued according to weights or measures entirely different from our own; we shall mention, for the time being, the English or Spanish pipe, the English pint, the English and American gallon, the French barrique, and the Turkish or Romanian oka.
