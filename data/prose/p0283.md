## Preparation of Syrups from Acidic Fruits (continued)

Adding to the juice a small amount of milk solids, which will coagulate quickly, or alternatively using egg white according to the instructions given above, or even by subjecting the juice itself to the action of boiling water after having sealed it in bottles.

Using refined sugar, and if the juice is well clarified, no foam will form on the surface of the syrup; however, if any bubbles appear, they should be removed before taking the syrup off the heat.

Everything we have said thus far applies to the preparation of all other acidic fruit syrups.

### Syrups of Currants, Bitter Oranges, Cherries, and Raspberries

These are prepared easily by using their respective preserved juices, which are poured (decanted and filtered) in the proportion of 5.2 kg per 10 kg of refined sugar, heating the mixture rapidly while stirring with a spatula. Once boiling ceases, remove from heat; let it rest for a moment, remove the foam that has formed, and finally strain through wool felt.

These syrups when hot should read 32°.

### Pomegranate Syrup

Heat to boiling 47 grams of ground cochineal with 1 liter of water, adding 10 grams of tartaric acid. Pour this solution into a syrup prepared according to the usual rules and clarified with 10 kg refined sugar and 5 liters of water; and finally add a solution of 22 grams citric acid and 18 grams tartaric acid in 500 cc of water. Heat to boiling; after cooling add 5–6 centiliters of vanilla tincture; finally strain through wool using paper pulp.
