The aforementioned cloth filters must be washed thoroughly when new, since otherwise they would impart particular flavors to the products filtered through them; indeed, it is a good idea to break them in by first filtering alcohol and syrup through them before using them for their final purpose.

Filtration of essential oils can be carried out using cotton inside a glass funnel: for this purpose, small glass beads (or glass fragments) are placed in the neck of the funnel, and then a layer of very fine carded cotton.

*Fig. 15.* — *Fig. 16.*

The liquid is poured along the wall of the funnel, not directly onto the cotton, since in that case the cotton would become soaked with the liquid and, clumping together, would lose much of its permeability. The cotton must be changed frequently, as it quickly becomes impregnated with impurities that clog its pores.

On the market there are also some special filters, known as bag filters, which are widely used and quite fast for filtering dense liquids such as syrups, liqueurs, etc.

It will suffice merely to mention them here, noting as particularly good those supplied by the Lucas Frères firm of Paris.
