# Testing the Purity of Ethyl Alcohol

The ratio in which the three different distillation products — bad-tasting heads, the heart, and bad-tasting tails — stand to one another varies according to the more or less perfected stills used in the industry; in any case, it can be said that rectification never yields the totality of the ethyl alcohol of the low wines in a state of purity, nor has this ideal result been achieved by the many other purification methods proposed up to now.

Even good-tasting commercial alcohol contains a certain quantity of contaminating compounds, which it would be too costly to eliminate completely.

Therefore, in the interest of industry more than of hygiene, almost all the States of Europe have agreed to establish, for the impurities of alcohol intended for consumption as a beverage, a tolerance limit accepted almost everywhere at 2 per thousand by volume of anhydrous alcohol.

The degree of these impurities varies in commercial alcohols according to the different industrial processes employed in distillation and rectification: the limit, according to the hygienic regulations in force in Italy, for an alcohol of 95° on the Gay-Lussac alcoholometer, is tolerated at 2%, as assessed according to the test using the Rose method, prescribed as official. However, since this test, although recognized as the most accurate, is very complicated and difficult — because several sources of error are possible in carrying it out — it cannot be performed except by a technical person, we shall point out some simple, almost empirical, procedures for approximately recognizing the overall presence of all impurities, and for detecting the most important ones.

**Test for ethyl alcohol (1).** — A simple test to establish the presence of traces of empyreumatic oils…

---

(1) The Official Pharmacopoeia of the Kingdom prescribes the following tests:

1) It must show a neutral reaction;

2) 10 cc diluted with an equal volume of water must not turn brown when heated with a few drops of a solution (1:19) of silver nitrate in distilled water mixed with enough ammonia to redissolve the precipitate that initially forms;

3) 3 cc rubbed between the hands must not give off an unpleasant odor (fusel/tail alcohol);

4) 5 cc evaporated in a small capsule over a water bath must not leave a weighable residue.
