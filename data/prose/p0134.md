## Falsifications of Essential Oils

As already mentioned in the notes given above regarding the essential oils previously listed, adulterated products of various kinds are quite often found on the market. Here are some practical means of recognizing such adulterations and falsifications in general:

**Alcohol.** — One can first recognize whether an essential oil has been adulterated with alcohol from its appearance, which becomes more fluid, and from its less intense odor; furthermore, by mixing it with an equal part of pure glycerin (density = 1.25) and shaking vigorously, if alcohol is present it will be drawn down into the glycerin, and the resulting increase in the glycerin's volume gives the measure of the quantity of alcohol contained.

In a graduated tube, equal volumes of essential oil and glycerin are shaken together, then left to rest for a few hours. If the essence contains alcohol, its volume will have decreased.

One can also proceed in the following way: the essential oil is placed in a small tube together with a small amount of thoroughly dried potassium acetate or calcium chloride; the mixture is then heated in a water bath for about 5 minutes while stirring. If the essential oil contains alcohol, a solution will form that separates out from the oil.

Another test, finally, was proposed by Hager, applicable to any essential oil, to detect adulteration with alcohol; it is based on the property of tannin to coagulate into a mass in the presence of alcohol, provided it is not otherwise altered within a pure essential oil.
