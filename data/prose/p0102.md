**Melilot.** — A native plant, very widespread in the wild, found in woods and meadows, where it blooms all summer long; it bears small, numerous yellow flowers in clusters; it is almost odorless when fresh, but on drying it acquires a strongly pronounced fragrance reminiscent of tonka bean.

**Melissa (Lemon Balm).** — *Melissa officinalis* is a perennial herb that grows wild in shady places; the whole plant, but especially the leaves when rubbed, gives off a pleasant odor similar to that of lemon; it has a slightly bitter, aromatic taste. It is commonly called Cedronella, lemon herb, etc.

**Bogbean (Menyanthes trifoliata).** — *Menyanthes trifoliata* (Gentianaceae) is also commonly known as "trifoglio fibrino" (fibrous trefoil) or water trefoil; it grows in marshy places, but it is not very common. The leaves are used; they are deeply divided into three oval, blunt-tipped segments, 5–7 cm long and 3–4 cm wide, odorless, and bitter in taste.

*M. piperita* (peppermint) — a perennial herb cultivated on a large scale in England and the United States, as well as in France, Italy, etc. The whole plant gives off a strong, pleasant, aromatic odor and has a pungent, aromatic taste that leaves a sensation of freshness; the stem, about 50 cm tall, is slightly velvety; the flowers are violet-colored. This is the species that yields the best essential oil, which accounts for its extensive cultivation. Its oval, toothed leaves must be picked when the plant is in full bloom and should not be kept for more than one year.

*M. vulgaris* (common mint, spearmint, or Roman mint) — cultivated in all parts of the world, though less abundantly than peppermint.
