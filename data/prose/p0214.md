# Preparations for Composing Liqueurs: Aromatic Waters by Distillation

# Aromatic Waters by Distillation

Under this designation are included all aromatic products in which the vehicle serving as solvent is plain water.

Distilled aromatic waters contain a certain quantity of volatile oil, which they have extracted from the substance with which they were brought into contact. In some cases, however, their fragrance is due to other substances; indeed, some highly odorous plants do not contain essential oils (mignonette, tuberose, jasmine, lily of the valley, etc.); these oils are therefore not the sole cause of fragrance in plants, as has already been noted.

In the composition of liqueurs, aromatic waters are very useful since they impart to the product a fine, delicate fragrance that could not be obtained with aromatic alcoholic preparations.

The preparation of such waters requires great care and much attention, as empyreumatic products very easily develop, which spoil the entire distilled product.

Distillation of aromatic waters is carried out indifferently by water bath or by means of steam current.

The distillation method, the fresh or dried state of the plant material, the quantity and temperature of the water all greatly influence the preparation of distilled waters and essential oils, and consequently the quality and quantity of the products obtained.
