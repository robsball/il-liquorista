Various classifications of liqueurs have been proposed, according to whether one considers their origin (natural or artificial), or their method of preparation (cold or hot process), or their alcoholic or sugar percentage (spirits, rosolios, creams, elixirs), or their quality (superfine, ordinary), etc.; likewise, distinct categories or groupings are included under special designations, such as Ratafias, Bitters (Amari), etc.

As for the particular or fanciful names given to each different liqueur, their variety is infinite and it is certainly not possible to list them all; moreover, the originality and eccentricity of some names do not serve to indicate any significant difference in character, since sometimes a new and coquettish label or a different coloring is enough to transform an already-known product into a 'new' liqueur.

We will briefly discuss these general, typical distinctions, and we will begin with the simplest classification:

1) Natural liqueurs;
2) Artificial liqueurs;
3) Aromatized wines.
