The purpose of the lime treatment is to destroy the germinative properties, which are however equally destroyed by simple sun-drying for just one week.

On the market one finds nutmegs blackened through poor storage or worm-eaten, or even depleted of their fat and essential oil.

**Oregano.** — Common oregano (Labiatae) is a well-known plant that grows in mountainous places; it bears small heart-shaped, velvety leaves of a deep green colour, and its flowers are reddish.

It has a pleasant aromatic odour and a bitter, aromatic taste, both due to the presence of an essential oil.

**Pimento.** — English pimento. Clove pepper. Jamaica pepper. It consists of the seeds of *Myrtus pimenta*, an evergreen tree of the West Indies. Most of what is found on the market comes from Jamaica; it appears as berries 4 to 6 mm in diameter, with a woody, fragile pericarp of rusty-brown colour, bearing small tubercles that contain essential oil and give it a rough appearance. The inside of the seed is divided into two chambers, each containing a kidney-shaped, brown seed with a large spiral embryo. Pimento fruits have a very pungent aromatic odour and taste, recalling clove, cinnamon, and nutmeg. The seed is less aromatic than the pericarp. This drug is often mixed with similar but less aromatic varieties of Mexican origin, either coarser-grained or of oval shape.

**Polypody.** — Oak polypody is a very common fern that grows especially at the base of old oak trees, of which the rhizome is sometimes used; it is woody, hard, about one centimetre thick, with numerous thread-like rootlets; internally it is greenish, with a taste that is sweetish at first, then bitter and nauseating.
