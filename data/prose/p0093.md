**Cinchona Bark (China).** — Cinchona bark is obtained from various plants of the Cinchonaceae family.

These are evergreen trees, native to South America (Venezuela, Ecuador, Peru, Colombia, and Bolivia). They are cultivated in Java, Ceylon, India, etc.

Cinchona barks are classified into various qualities according to color:

1°. **Pale Cinchona (China pallida, or "reale")** is obtained from young trees of *Cinchona officinalis*; it is found in commerce in tubes about 30 cm long by 5–6 mm in diameter, with a thickness of 1–2 mm; they are brownish in color with silvery-gray spots, and often bear patches of lichen. The surface is rough. The inner face is finely striated and pale yellow. It has a distinctive, pronounced odor, with an astringent and bitter taste.

2°. **Yellow Cinchona (China gialla)** is obtained from *Cinchona calisaya*; it is the most important for medicinal uses. It is found either flat or in tubes. Flat Calisaya comes in irregular pieces about 30 cm by 8–10 cm, 4–8 mm thick. It is orange-brown or rust colored, with darker spots on the outer face. Tubular Calisaya, often double-layered, has a diameter of 2–3 cm, and is covered with a silvery-gray, wrinkled cork layer with longitudinal cracks; this layer easily peels away to reveal a cinnamon-colored surface beneath. The inner face is dark brown, with fine fibers.

This bark is sometimes found in very small tubes, in which case it cannot be distinguished from pale cinchona (see No. 1).

3°. **Red Cinchona (China rossa)** is obtained from *Cinchona succirubra*, and is considerably less important than the previous varieties. It is extensively cultivated in Ceylon. It comes in pieces more than 1 cm thick.
