# Materials and the Laboratory

We will give a brief list of the principal utensils with which the liqueur-maker's laboratory should be equipped; we will not dwell too much on this subject because, generally speaking, everyone does as they can and as they see fit in this regard.

**1°** A still and its corresponding furnace, arranged for steam heating, since for large-scale liqueur production it is advisable to install a steam generator, which can also be used for concentrating juices and syrups, as well as for motive power for grinding mills, etc.

In the chapter on Distillation, the various types of stills and their operation are described.

**2°** Tinned-copper basins with rounded bottoms, very wide and shallow, boilers and tubs for mixtures; tinned-copper or iron pots for cooking syrups.

**3°** Displacement apparatus (see Maceration) for preparing tinctures; demijohns of various sizes or wide-necked glass containers for macerations.

**4°** Filters of various sizes — of wool, of felt, hood-shaped (see Filtration), of cloth and of paper, smooth and pleated. It is advisable to mark the fabric filters so as to recognize them, in order to always use them for the same type of liqueur being filtered.

**5°** Funnels of various capacities, of glass, for paper filtration; funnels of tin, tinned copper, or aluminum, some with lids, and wooden frames and stands to hold the wool filters.

**6°** Mortars of iron, stone, or porcelain.

**7°** A roaster for toasting seeds.
