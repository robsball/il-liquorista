# Filtration (continued)

To obtain a filtered liquid, one must use paper filters and glass funnels. Filter paper is available commercially in various qualities; some grey papers are not very resistant, but allow a rapid and quite satisfactory filtration. Fine white papers, intended for chemical uses, are more expensive but should be preferred for fine liqueurs.

For some time now it has also become common practice to reinforce filter paper by subjecting it to an initial transformation into vegetable parchment. To do this, the paper is dipped for a few moments in nitric acid of density 1.41, and immediately afterward washed abundantly.

*Fig. 17.*

This treatment makes the paper considerably more resistant, so that it can be washed repeatedly without damage; as for permeability, it is only slightly reduced.

Paper filters are made (fig. 17) in the usual way, with pleats, taking care that the tip is perfectly conical and smooth and does not form a little pocket, in which case the filter would be much more likely to tear.

In certain cases, when the residue left by the liquid needs to be reused, it is better to use smooth (unpleated) filters, obtained simply by folding a rounded sheet of filter paper in four.

It will not be superfluous to recall a few further precautions suited to ensuring good filtration, which — although apparently a very easy operation — can in practice present some unforeseen difficulties.

To guard against the bottom of the filter tearing, one can fit inside it another very small filter, acting as a counter-point (reinforcement). Care must be taken that the funnel is perfectly dry before fitting the filter into it, because wet spots could easily cause tears. When pouring in the liquid, it should be made to run down along the walls of the filter, not directly onto the bottom, and the filter should not be filled to excess; rather, a space of one to two centimeters should be left between the rim of the filter and the level of the liquid. The filter itself should also sit a few millimeters lower than the rim of the funnel.
