The second (fig. 7) is called the Deroy Rapid-Circulation Still. It is entirely similar to those already described, except for a simple device which serves to keep the boiling mass continuously agitated, producing rapid and uniform distillation.

![Fig. 8]

It consists of a double bottom, in the lower part of which is fixed a tube (18), which carries at its upper end a perforated copper plate; to prevent this double bottom from resting directly on the bottom of the boiler, a copper grid is interposed between them. Here now is how this apparatus works. As soon as the boiling of the liquid contained between the two bottoms begins, it rises through tube 18, at the end of which (owing to the perforated plate) it must spread out over a disc, also perforated (19), from which it drips down with uniform distribution onto the mass below. This circulating movement of the liquid remains constant throughout the course of the distillation, and one thus obtains, as has been said, excellent results.
