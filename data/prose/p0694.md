# Hydromel

## Hydromel

This is a beverage of very ancient use: it can be prepared following different procedures. We shall limit ourselves to mentioning one of the most common.

Heat gently over low heat for 1–1¼ hours, skimming frequently, 1 part honey with 10–11 parts distilled water until the mixture becomes clear. One must avoid excessive heating, as this could spoil the aroma of the honey. Allow to cool, transfer to a barrel, preferably one already in use, filling it to the bung hole, which is left open. Abandon the whole to fermentation, which proceeds more rapidly the higher the temperature of the room; if desired, it can be accelerated with a little yeast. Fermentation is complete in 10–12 weeks. Close the bung hole and allow to rest for 2 to 4 months. After this time the hydromel is finished and is poured into bottles.

The longer the fermentation, the more aromatic the flavor of the hydromel becomes. A little cream of tartar can also be added.

A most exquisite hydromel is obtained by using, instead of water, wine must. The aroma can be intensified by adding aromatic substances such as the following:

1. Hops
2. Cloves 40, nutmeg 15, cinnamon 5, mace 3, anise 15, fennel 12
3. Galangal 20, gentian root 10, sage leaves 5, cinnamon 30
4. Nutmeg 30, cinnamon 25
5. Thyme

Boil the mixture in one liter of honey water, strain, and pour into the barrel.

When filling bottles, be sure to leave an empty space of at least 3 centimeters between the liquid and the stopper.
