# Juniper Aquavit / Gin

From this malt extract, 1.200 liters are employed, mixed with 4.200 liters of alcohol at 90°, 4.500 liters of water, and 1 liter of sugar syrup to obtain a product with an alcohol content of 45% ABV and 1% sugar.

## Rye Aquavit from Alborg (Alborger Tafel Korn)

The aromatic spirit is prepared by macerating for a few days according to the general methods indicated:

- Crushed rye, lightly roasted: 750 g
- Crushed carob pods: 250 g
- Crushed coriander: 25 g

Mixed in 4 liters of alcohol at 90° and 2 liters of water.

Add 6 liters of this spirit to 18.250 liters of alcohol at 90°, fortified with 1 g of juniper essential oil, and finally mix in 25 liters of water and 500 g of sugar syrup containing 500 g of sugar.

This yields 50 liters of product with an alcohol content of 40% ABV.

## Juniper Aquavit or Gin

It is an aquavit from cereals distilled in the presence of crushed juniper berries; it is very widespread in Holland and England. When necessary, it is diluted with the purest alcohol and corrected by the addition of nitric ether (30 g per hectoliter) and acetic ether (150 g per hectoliter), and aromatized with other ingredients (angelica, coriander, etc.).

Since in this manual we will subsequently indicate several recipes for the preparation of juniper liqueurs, we will not dwell on this subject, observing only that with the mixtures now described, various types of this aquavit can be obtained by omitting the use of sugar or reducing it to only a very small proportion (1 kg per hectoliter).
