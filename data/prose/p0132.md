# Neroli or Orange Blossom

**Neroli or orange blossom.** — The essential oil of fresh orange blossom is colorless, but soon turns red in light; it is also known by the name of oil or essence of Neroli. By distillation of the petals of various neroli or orange blossoms, an essential oil of intense and delicate perfume is obtained: several qualities are distinguished according to their origin:

1° **Neroli Bigarade**, the most prized, is extracted from the fresh flowers of the *Citrus vulgaris* or Bigaradia, in a proportion of about 0.1%. The fresh product is nearly colorless, but in light it gradually turns reddish-brown. It is easily soluble in alcohol, and also slightly soluble in water.

2° A less prized variety, though still excellent, is likewise obtained in a proportion of 0.1% by distillation from the fresh flowers of the sweet orange (Portugal).

The water with which the essential oil was carried over during distillation, after the oil has been separated, constitutes **Orange Blossom Water**, which finds very wide use in perfumery and in the manufacture of liqueurs; to preserve it unaltered for a long time, it is advisable to mix in a little alcohol.

3° By steam-distilling the unripe fruits, leaves, and buds of *Citrus Bigaradia* (bitter orange), an inferior quality of Neroli essence is obtained, called **Petit Grain**; it contains almost the same components as Neroli essence, but has a less delicate perfume: it is therefore often used to cut the finer product. Likewise, to adulterate the latter — which is quite expensive — bergamot essential oil, oil of turpentine, copaiba balsam, castor oil, and alcohol, among others, are employed.

**Nerolina** is a solid residue obtained in crystalline form upon cooling the essence of orange blossoms; it retains the scent to a very high degree. A product with a very similar odor has been obtained by chemical synthesis, namely the ethyl ether of beta-naphthol.

**Nutmeg.** — This oil is contained in the seeds in a proportion of about 10% and is obtained especially from the small, broken ones. It is soluble in 5 parts of alcohol. It has the same uses as oil of mace.

**Origano.** — This oil is straw-yellow in color and darkens with time. It dissolves easily in alcohol.
