**Bitter Orange.** — This essential oil is obtained by pressing or squeezing the peels of bitter oranges, which give the best product, as well as from those of sweet oranges. It is a free-flowing, yellowish liquid, which over time becomes denser and darker. It has a strong, aromatic odor.

In odor and color it differs little from the essential oil of sweet oranges, but much more in flavor, which is distinctly bitter, and the more bitter the fresher the essence is. This bitterness is partly due to the mucilage contained in the pressed essential oil; with aging it diminishes noticeably.

Although this essence is obtained by pressing rather than by distillation, its flavor cannot be compared to that of the alcoholic tincture prepared from dried peels. This tincture contains another bitter substance that is essential for fine orange liqueurs.

Considering that the tincture can be prepared at lower cost, that it immediately yields clear liqueurs ready for the market, whereas the essence — being quite difficultly soluble — clouds the mixtures, and moreover there is the risk of receiving old essences or ones cut with Portugal essence, there is no reason to prefer the use of the essence over that of the tincture.

In order to make this essence more easily soluble, attempts have been made to rectify it. This goal is indeed achieved, but most of the bitter substance remains in the still, and the flavor of the essence is thereby worsened.

The same applies to the so-called essences of orange peel and curaçao, which are distilled in the same way as citron peel essence. All these essences lack the bitterness characteristic of orange, which can only be obtained by extraction from the peels using alcohol.

**Sweet Orange (Essential oil of Portugal).** — This is obtained from the peels of many species of Citrus aurantium, but especially of the Portugal orange, by pressing. It has a more yellow color than citron essence, and a pleasant, fresh odor.
