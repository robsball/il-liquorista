# Coloring Agents (continued): Carmine, Turmeric, Phytolacca, Indigo

The various color grades are distinguished by numbers, of which the highest is No. 40. For coloring liqueurs and confections it is mixed with sugar in the form of a syrup.

A special product found on the market is liquid Carmine for beverages, made up of 80 g of cochineal carmine, 20 g of ammonia, and 2 liters of simple syrup.

On the market there are also some substitutes for carmine, consisting of mixtures of aniline dyes, which need not be discussed here.

**Turmeric.** — This is the root of *Curcuma longa*, coming from India and from China, where it grows wild. Several commercial qualities are distinguished according to origin: in rounded pieces the size of a hazelnut (round turmeric), or in elongated pieces of 5–6 cm, irregular, with circular rings. It has a strong odor and an aromatic taste; it contains a golden-yellow, translucent resinous substance, soluble in alcohol.

**Phytolacca (Amaranth/Pokeweed).** — The berries of this plant, common throughout Europe (*Phytolacca decandra*), are violet-black in color and contain a seed of intense red color. They are used fresh to color liqueurs, syrups, etc. On the market one finds Phytolacca syrup, or Amaranth syrup, very commonly used in confectionery. This color, however, is poorly suited to liqueurs of high alcoholic strength. (See also Kermes.)

**Indigo.** — This substance comes from India, from various indigo-bearing plants, and is sold in the form of uneven compressed cakes which, when crushed, show a metallic iridescence if the product is of good quality. Several commercial grades are known, and it is also prepared synthetically by various chemical processes. Indigo consists of a mixture of various mineral and organic substances, the most important of which is Indigotin, or Indigo Blue. It is insoluble in water and in alcohol, only slightly soluble in boiling alcohol; it dissolves in glacial acetic acid and in fuming sulfuric acid.

We will describe in a special chapter the methods for preparing its solutions.
