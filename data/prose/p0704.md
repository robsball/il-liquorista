### Determination of Sugar Content

Boiling. When the contents of the capsule have become dense, the temperature is raised to 110°C and heating is continued for 15 minutes, after which the capsule is allowed to cool and is weighed. The weight of the contents, which consists almost exclusively of pure sugar, indicates with sufficient precision the percentage of sugar contained in the liquor.

One may also make use of the Balling saccharometer, which is graduated such that in a solution containing 10, 20, 30 grams of sugar, it reads 10°, 20°, 30°, etc.

By proceeding in the manner described, it is possible to arrive at knowing with sufficient precision the quantities of alcohol and sugar contained in a liquor; but with regard to essential oils, one can arrive at knowing their qualities but not their quantities.

Consequently, to formulate a liquor it is necessary to proceed by repeated trials until the desired result is achieved.
