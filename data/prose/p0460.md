# On the Preparation of Liqueurs Using Essences

Since we are dealing with substances endowed with pronounced and characteristic flavors, individual judgment regarding the refinement of their mixtures has only relative value. The very same liqueur that proves excellent when prepared by maceration to one palate may prove disgusting to another, who would prefer it prepared by means of essences, or vice versa.

On these idiosyncrasies of taste it is not the place here to debate, nor would we likely arrive at any useful conclusion by dwelling on them. The palate does, however, have general norms common to the great majority of individuals, at least within a given region, and other even more universal norms from which one absolutely cannot deviate. One has good taste if they do not make exceptions to these general norms. And whoever feels they must make exceptions to them, even if for the better—as is naturally always the conviction of those in such a situation—should not dedicate themselves to the preparation of products that must obtain the approval of the majority.

Regarding the preparation of liqueurs by means of essences, nothing could be simpler, since it is merely a matter of dissolving a given quantity of essential oils or compound essences in alcohol and then diluting with sugar syrup or with water, adding afterward the sugar, color, etc.

With equal fineness of the essential oils employed, the quality of the liqueur depends on the quantity of such essences used, as well as on that of the alcohol and water.

At the end of the chapter we will present a collection of recipes of very easy execution, according to which the essential oils are directly added to a mixture composed of alcohol, water, and sugar, termed Simple Liqueur.

The essential oils, carefully weighed or measured by drops using a dropper, are introduced into an appropriate vessel, into which a portion of the alcohol is poured, then agitated vigorously to obtain perfect dissolution; the other substances are then added, and finally the usual operations of clarification, filtration, coloring, etc., are proceeded with.

To use essential oils by the drop, we employ, as we have mentioned, a dropper.
