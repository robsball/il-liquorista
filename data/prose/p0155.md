# Properties of Coloring Agents Used in Liqueur-Making

| Name of the Colorant | Color of the Solid Matter | Color of the Solution | Soluble in |
|---|---|---|---|
| Azoflavine (Azoyellow, Indian yellow, Citronin, Acid Yellow) | Ochre-yellow | Yellow | Hot water |
| Lyon Blue (Fuchsine Blue) | Green-gray needles | Blue | Water |
| Methylene Blue | Reddish-brown powder | Intense blue (in alcohol); blue (in water) | Alcohol; water |
| Bordeaux (found in several shades) | Reddish-brown | Amaranth red; reddish-brown | Water |
| Chrysoidine | Reddish-brown powder | Brown; reddish-brown | Water |
| Eosin | Reddish-blue crystals; reddish-brown powder | Dilute solution: greenish-yellow | Alcohol |
| Erythrosine | Brown | Cherry red | Water |
| Sulfonated Fuchsin (Acid Fuchsin) | Metallic green powder | Violet-red | Alcohol (very little); water |
| Ponceau (found in many shades) | Red | More or less vivid red | Water |
| Roccelline (stable red) (Cerasine, Orcelline, Rubidine) | Reddish-brown | Deep red | Cold water, better in hot water |
| Biebrich Scarlet | Reddish-brown | Orange-red | Water |
| Tropeolin O (Acid Yellow, Orange, etc.) | Yellow-brown | Orange | Water |
| Malachite Green | Brownish-green | Vivid green | Water |
| Paris Violet (Methyl Violet / Methylaniline Violet) | Metallic green | Intense violet | Water |
