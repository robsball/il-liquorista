# General principles for extract preparation and use

Moreover, they must be protected from air and light. They keep better in a moderately warm location and should only be filtered when necessary.

Extracts can also be prepared using ethereal oils appropriately combined: in this case they are obtained easily by diluting in alcohol the compound essences or ethereal oils already indicated. Generally 1 part of these essences is diluted with 10 parts of alcohol at 96°, and of the extract thus obtained, 2 grams per litre is used to flavour a litre of liqueur.

Some extracts finally, very complex, are composed of tinctures prepared from mixtures of plant materials with the addition of ethereal oils or essences.

We shall now present a varied series of these extracts for liqueurs, grouped according to their different composition, or precisely into:

1. **Extracts properly so called or concentrated tinctures** obtained directly from one or from a mixture of plant materials, by means of maceration or digestion;

2. **Extracts obtained by mixing essential oils**, combined with alcohol or essences;

3. **Composite extracts** by mixing tinctures composed of plant materials and essential oils or essences.

For some of these products we shall provide instructions for the composition of the respective liqueur. For the others in general the table we present further on will serve as a standard: however we observe that the most important criterion is that of personal taste or the particular conditions, purposes and uses of the manufacture.
