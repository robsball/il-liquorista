# Standardization of Vermouth Wine and Alcoholization Table

The wine to be used must conform to a single type through appropriate corrections of alcohol content, sugar, and acidity, as much as possible. Since wines, even from the same locality, can vary in their principal components according to vintage and harvest conditions, it is an essential requirement to maintain constant the type of the vermouth product.

**Example:** To bring a wine of only 10° alcoholic strength to 17°, one must add to it, per hectoliter, 9.45 liters of rectified alcohol at 95°.

The sugar content of vermouth varies from 10 to 16%, and is obtained roughly half by adding sugar to the wine. Naturally, for the best result of the product, this sugar should always be cane sugar; some producers also employ glucose.

As regards bitter flavor and aroma, these should not be excessively pronounced, so as not to turn an agreeable beverage into a medicament.
