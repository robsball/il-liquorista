# Essential Oils (continued): Fennel, Galangal, Hyssop, Cloves, Juniper

...from the stems. The rectified oil is barely yellowish, but darkens with age. Its odor recalls that of fennel; its taste is sweetish and slightly aromatic. It is often adulterated with turpentine essence and alcohol, or sold after being stripped of its anethole, of which it normally contains about 60 to 70%.

It readily solidifies into crystals, especially the denser product obtained in the second stage of distillation, whereas the product from the first stage is more fluid, more volatile, and less easily crystallized.

**Galangal.** — The oil obtained by extraction from galangal is a light greenish color and darkens with age; for its other characteristics see Juniper. It is very little used.

**Hyssop.** — The oil obtained from this plant is colorless, but darkens rapidly with time or exposure to air.

It is readily soluble in alcohol.

**Cloves.** — Cloves contain 15 to 18% essential oil, which when freshly prepared is yellowish and dense; with time it darkens further. It has a strong, characteristic odor and a very aromatic, burning taste. It is composed of about 90% eugenol, the remainder being a terpene along with traces of vanillin. It is often adulterated with copaiba balsam, cedarwood oil, almond oil, castor oil, alcohol, turpentine, etc.

**Juniper (Berries).** — Obtained from juniper berries (*Juniperus communis*).

The essential oil (or juniper oil) is colorless and very fluid when freshly prepared; with time it darkens and becomes viscous. It is often adulterated with turpentine essence distilled over juniper branches. It is also used in medicine for external application.

Distillation of this essential oil requires a high temperature, so that only the fraction that distills over first resembles juniper berries in flavor and aroma and can be used for liqueurs. The less volatile fractions resemble turpentine essence and are separated out as 'juniper wood essence' for other uses. The quality of the oil therefore depends on proper separation of the distillate. However, even the best essential oil cannot substitute for a spirit tincture distilled directly over juniper berries.
