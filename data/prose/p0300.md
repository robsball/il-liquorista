# Azure (Indigo Blue)

The most commonly used is indigo (see p. 140); a solution is prepared with:

- Dried indigo powder: 50 g
- Pure concentrated sulfuric acid: 400 g

Mix in a porcelain capsule and leave the mixture for 5–6 days, stirring frequently with a glass rod. Then slowly pour this solution into two liters of distilled water (do not reverse the order!) in a wide maiolica or porcelain vessel and leave it for another day. Finally, neutralize by carefully adding in small portions 150 g (or 300 g) of calcium carbonate to the mixture. Once effervescence ceases, leave at rest, then decant the liquid, add 240 g of 90° alcohol, and finally filter through paper.

One may also directly employ indigo extract or indigo paste obtained commercially to prepare the tincture. Mix:

- Indigo extract: 100 g
- 90° alcohol: 200 g
- Distilled water: 600 g

Leave the mixture until dissolved, then filter through paper.

If you wish to use directly a 5% tincture of indigo carmine, dissolve the indigo carmine powder finely (5 g) in distilled water (80 g), then add 90° alcohol (25 g); then filter.

A longer procedure, which yields a better product, consists in treating indigo with sulfuric acid as above; then pour the liquid while continuously stirring into 8 liters of distilled water. Immerse pieces of molleton cloth or wool scraps in this and bring to a boil: the fabric takes up the coloring matter. Then wash thoroughly with large quantities of water to remove the acidity. Next boil with 6 liters of water made alkaline with 5 g of potassium carbonate and wash the fabric well—it will release the coloring substance into the water. Filter through paper, add 700 g of 90° alcohol, and filter again.
