# Sugar Syrup Clarification and Decolorization

## Clarification

For 50 kg of raw sugar, take 8 fresh egg whites, place them in a copper bowl with their shells, and beat them thoroughly with one liter of water. Then gradually add 7 liters of water while stirring.

Instead of egg whites (of which approximately one is used per 10 kg of sugar), dried albumin can be used, reconstituted in water, calculating that 11–4 grams of this corresponds to approximately one egg white.

Place 50 kg of raw sugar of good quality in an unlined copper bowl, add 20 liters of water and albumin solution, stirring with a wooden spatula until the sugar is completely dissolved. Bring rapidly to a boil, pour in one liter of albumin solution, repeat the operation, and remove from heat. Skim off the foam, then return to the fire. When the mass is boiling, add three liters of water in several portions, skimming each time. When the syrup is completely clear and free of foam, pass it through a cloth filter. It should then measure 31° Baumé.

## Decolorization

When using colored sugar, it is necessary to decolorize it, which in many cases can be done simultaneously with clarification, in which case the following proportions are followed:

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Sugar | 90 kg |
| Water | 45 kg |
| Wood charcoal | 1.5 kg |
| Bone black powder | 3 kg |
| Egg whites | 8 no. |

Beat the egg whites with water and pour them into the sugar placed in a bowl with the remaining albumin solution, keeping 2/3 of this for clarification. Heat while stirring constantly, and when all the sugar is dissolved, add the bone black and charcoal. When the syrup boils, pour in the reserved albumin solution, bring to a boil again, then remove the bowl from the heat. After sufficient rest, skim and filter through flannel.
