# Green Dye, Direct Green Tint, Intense Green Tint for Liqueurs, and Violet

## II. Green Dye

One can also prepare a green dye by dissolving 50 g of indigo carmine in 1 litre of distilled hot water and adding gradually to this solution the tincture of saffron or calendula (see above) until a beautiful green coloration is obtained. The liquid is filtered and 200 g of simple syrup is added.

## III. Direct Green Tint

To obtain a direct green tint, one can prepare it with plant leaves (lemon balm, spinach, hyssop, periwinkle, or bramble): they are finely pounded in a mortar, then the mass obtained is introduced into a wide-mouthed bottle, adding a double weight of alcohol at 90°. Shake vigorously and after 15 days of maceration, press or filter the tincture.

This color has the drawback of altering easily when exposed to light, turning yellow with turbidity of the liquor and formation of sediment. It was found that it becomes more stable by adding a weak solution of sodium carbonate, which is then saturated with hydrochloric acid.

## IV. Intense Green Tint for Liqueurs

Here is how one can prepare an intense green tint for liqueurs: Macerate for 21 hours, spinach or fresh herbs, finely chopped, 100 g in 1 litre of distilled water, with the addition of 1 g of crystalline sodium carbonate; press and discard the liquid obtained; pour over the plant residue 200 g of alcohol (70%), and after 12 hours (no longer) collect, by pressing and filtering, the tincture.

The solution is of a beautiful green color and harmless, and must be preserved in opaque glass vessels.

For the preparation of Chlorophyll, see Section on Coloring Matters, page 137.
