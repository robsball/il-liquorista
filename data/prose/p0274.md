## Rational Cooking of Sugar

We shall outline the simple procedure, adopted especially in France, for rapidly obtaining a concentrated syrup corresponding to an equal weight of sugar.

In a tinned copper kettle, clean and well-polished, one mixes 1000 parts refined sugar, or alternatively candied sugar, 2 parts chemically pure citric acid, and 500 parts water. Heat is applied over direct flame, stirring the mixture thoroughly so that the sugar may completely dissolve before the mass comes to a boil.

Remove with a skimming ladle the foam that forms at the surface of the syrup, until perfect clarity is achieved.

Prolong the boiling until the syrup begins to thicken, or until large bubbles evolve from within the mass while long threads form when lifting the skimming ladle; to reach this stage generally requires about 20 minutes.
