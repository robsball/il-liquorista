# Purification of Essential Oil Alcoholates (Continued)

During this time, the second demijohn will also have filled with distillate.

The contents of this demijohn are then treated precisely as described for the first, while a third demijohn is placed beneath the apparatus to proceed as with the previous ones. Overall, approximately 8 demijohns are required to remove the turbidifying substance.

A reliable indication that the treatment has been completed is when the alcohol distills clear.

To verify this, one fills a glass cylinder with this alcohol and observes it by transparency. If the liquid still appears turbid, distillation is continued.

If the distilled liquid continues to be turbid, one tests whether adding alcohol at 90° leaves it still turbid or renders it clear. In the latter case, a Florentine bottle is attached to the apparatus and indirect steam is passed over it rather strongly, so as to obtain the concentrated essential oil that does not turbidify.

When all the essential oil has been distilled from the apparatus, another 30 liters of weak alcohol are introduced into the still and approximately 25 liters are cautiously distilled in order to obtain a higher strength product.

This alcohol can be used in a subsequent distillation or sold as a non-turbidifying cumin alcoholate with oils.

The yield of essential oil is approximately 40–55%, provided that the cumin essential oil has been absolutely pure.

In the same manner, other simple extra-strong non-turbidifying ethereal oils are prepared.
