# Compound Essences

However, the final product, in volume, will turn out somewhat inferior to what would result from mixing the essences by weight. What matters is that the mixture be carried out entirely either by weight or by volume measurement.

In certain cases, when the weight to be measured is less than ten grams, one can use a common dropper, keeping in mind that on average 26 drops of essential oil correspond to 1 gram. Exceptions are made for clove, cinnamon, and bitter almond oils, which having greater density are calculated as 20 drops = 1 gram.

When it is not a matter of essential oils, this will be specified.
