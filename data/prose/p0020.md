And it is precisely here that the liquorist's art reveals itself: in knowing how to combine the various aromas appropriately, so as to obtain pleasant products of consistent composition.

The quality and clarity of the liqueurs obtained depend as much on the care taken in the manipulations needed to arrange and prepare the materials as on the choice of substances used in manufacture.

It is therefore absolutely essential to use first-quality alcohol, purified and rectified; refined sugar; distilled or chemically pure water; fresh, well-preserved plants, roots, flowers, and drugs; pure essential oils, essences and tinctures; irreproachable fruits and fruit juices; and coloring substances that are entirely harmless.

The liqueur manufacturer must above all be expert in the knowledge of raw materials, their characteristics and properties, and for vegetable substances he should be less concerned with their price than with their quality.

The good liquorist should not limit himself to following recipes exactly. These are meant only as a guide. He must be able to recognize which substances will yield pleasant compounds, must know how to take advantage of local products, and must be able to correct or enhance the aroma of a liqueur with suitable additions or modifications.

Often the fragrance of certain substances does not fully emerge unless combined with others, by which it may also be corrected. A few examples will suffice: amber alone has little fragrance, but combined with a minimal dose of musk, it develops fully; clove corrects cinnamon; green anise corrects star anise; vanilla ground with sugar acquires a more intense fragrance than alone, etc. (See the chapter on Spirit Infusions.)

From this arises the liquorist's need to be equipped with an abundant assortment of aromatic products, which is evidently not well suited to small-scale operations. However, a large plant is not always necessary, in which the personal touch would be lost. We stand at the middle ground, which combines the advantages of both extremes and excludes almost all of their drawbacks.
