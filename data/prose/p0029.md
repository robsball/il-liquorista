The preparation of alcohol is an industrial operation: alcohol is always formed when the so-called alcoholic fermentation of sugary substances occurs, or, more precisely, of glucose, which constitutes almost the entirety of the sugar contained in fruits or which is produced by saccharification in cereals, etc. As raw materials one employs either sugary substances, whether or not directly fermentable, or substances which, through suitable treatments, break down to yield fermentable sugary matter (such as starch), or fermented liquids which already contain alcohol (such as wine).

However, the scope and limits of this manual do not allow us to dwell at length on the study of raw materials or to follow their processing through the distinct stages in which it unfolds; nor on the industrial procedures adopted, nor on the description of the various apparatuses for the distillation and rectification of this product, since even a summary treatment would require an extensive chapter; moreover, the technical literature includes several specialized and detailed works, to which we refer those who wish to acquire the necessary information (1).
