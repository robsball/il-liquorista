# Equipment (continued)

**8.** A grinder or even a crushing mill.

**9.** A press (juice-extractor) for extracting the juice from fruits and for pressing the plant material after maceration (fig. 1): several cloth bags are needed to go with the press.

**10.** Some large and small sieves, with both fine and wide mesh, for decanting the liquids of tinctures away from the plant material and for separating powders.

**11.** One large scale and one small, very sensitive scale, for weighing drugs, essences, etc.

**12.** Graduated cylindrical vessels and containers, for measuring the volume of liquids and for mixtures; in addition, some dropper bottles for essences.

**13.** A Gay-Lussac or Tralles centesimal alcoholometer (or a Cartier spirit-weigher): it is advisable to own a highly accurate, officially certified one fitted with a thermometer, for checking the alcohol content of spirits purchased commercially (see page 37). Also hydrometers (syrup-weighers, wine-weighers, etc.) and Baumé areometers (see the Syrups chapter), etc.

**14.** A few siphons or several glass pipettes; tasting tubes.

In this regard, it would be no bad thing for the liquor-maker to keep on hand, with the necessary precautions, the main reagents in solution used for simple and easy tests of the products purchased commercially (alcohol, sugar, essential oils, etc.), which are described later on in this volume.

**15.** Secondary utensils (skimmers, ladles, spatulas...)
