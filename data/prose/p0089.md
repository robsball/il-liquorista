**Benzoin.** — This is a natural balsam collected by making incisions in the bark of *Styrax Benzoe*, a tree native to the West Indies, and it comes to market in wooden crates. Depending on its origin, several types are distinguished: Siam benzoin, with a pleasant vanilla-like odor; Sumatra benzoin, with an odor similar to that of storax; and Penang benzoin.

Three commercial grades of benzoin are also distinguished:

1. **Benzoin in tears** — pear-shaped, reddish grains; the most highly prized.
2. **Almond (amygdaloid) benzoin** (Malacca/Cambodian) — in shapeless, friable, grayish masses, made up of white almond-shaped fragments cemented together by a brown, porous paste.
3. **Bulk (sorted) benzoin** — in brownish masses, lacking the characteristic white patches of the previous grade, or mixed with plant debris.

Benzoin is soluble in alcohol; when heated it melts and burns, giving off fragrant white fumes. It contains resin, benzoic acid, traces of vanillin, etc.

**Bergamot.** — Bergamots are the fruits of *Citrus Bergamia*, a variety belonging to the lemon genus.

Two varieties are known and cultivated: *Citrus Bergamia vulgaris* (common bergamot), whose pear-shaped fruits are yellow and contain 10–15 yellow-greenish, acrid, inedible segments; and *Citrus Bergamia mellarosa* (Reggio or Siderno type), which is more highly regarded.

These fruits have a very pleasant and intense aroma and contain a slightly acidic juice. They are cultivated especially for the production of the essential oil, which is extracted from the peel by pressing.

**Cacao.** — This is the common name given to the seeds of *Theobroma Cacao*, a tree native to Central America and now cultivated in the Antilles, Mexico, Guatemala, Venezuela, Ecuador, the Philippines, Java, Borneo, etc.

The finest commercial qualities are Soconusco (or Xoconusco, from Guatemala) and Esmeralda (from Ecuador), though these are almost never found in Europe. The most common qualities available here are those from Caracas (Venezuela), Guayaquil (Ecuador), Surinam (Dutch Guiana), Maracaibo, the Antilles, Brazil, etc.
