# Flavor Classification and Artificial Aromas

**Sweet taste.** — Common anise (seeds), star anise (seeds), orange peel, carob, figs, fennel (seeds), manna.

**Bitter almond taste.** — Apricot kernels, cherry-laurel leaves, peach kernels, bitter almonds.

As far as plant materials are concerned, the best way to judge the intensity of their bitterness and their aromatic properties is based on practical experience.

We will leave aside — since it is not within the liquor-maker's competence — the classification and grouping of aromatic substances according to their medicinal and therapeutic properties. We will only mention that Aloe, Agaric, Rhubarb, Buckthorn (Frangula), and also Saffron have a purgative and drastic action; while Coca, Chamomile, etc., are calming; Coffee, Kola nut, Tea, etc., are stimulants of the nervous system; and Cinchona (China) and Gentian are tonics, etc. (1)

---

**Artificial Aromas or Perfumes.** — The enumeration of aromatic substances, and even more so of the odoriferous principles extracted from them, would now lead us to also discuss synthetic artificial products — that is, those products which the chemical industry, now so advanced, has managed to obtain through synthesis, using raw materials of organic origin and complex processes; such products are similar in properties, in chemical composition, and in scent to the natural ones.

We have already mentioned Ionone and Vanillin; in addition to these we may mention Nerolin, Aubépine, Geraniol, Heliotropin, Coumarin, Citral, Anethole, etc., as well as several artificial essential oils.

We must note, however, that these products generally find application in perfumery rather than in liquor-making, while their use still leaves much to be desired as regards the quality and stability of the scent.
