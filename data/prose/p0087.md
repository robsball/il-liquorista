From orange blossoms one obtains, by distillation, orange-flower water and an essence called Neroli, although its aroma is not as delicate as that of the fresh flowers.

The fruit of the Bitter Orange, or Curaçao orange, or Melangolo (in Sicilian, *arancio agro*, from *sprimiri*, *cartàsi*) resembles the sweet orange in every respect except for its juice, which is sour or bitter. It is sold commercially in brine, or used in the preparation of candied fruit as well as of many bitters (Bitters), Curaçao, etc.

Chinotti are small bitter oranges (Bigarade), green and unripe, picked before full maturity, which reach the market candied in crates: the ones chosen are those of light green color, glacé-finished, and firm in texture; those of dark green color are of lesser quality; those with a blond hue are yellowish, unattractive in appearance, and soft in texture.

From the leaves of this tree an essential oil is extracted, called in French *Petit grain*, because it used to be extracted from the rind of the still very small fruits.

**Arnica.** — *Arnica montana* grows in the mountain meadows of central Europe. Its flowers have the usual daisy-like shape, are golden yellow, and have a faint but pleasant scent. The root is a cylindrical rhizome, dark brown, with secondary rootlets. The whole plant, but especially the flowers, contains a bitter substance, Arnicin, and an essential oil.

**Arum.** — *Arum maculatum*, commonly called Cuckoo-pint, is a herbaceous plant that grows in shaded places throughout Europe; it is easily recognized by its large, smooth, dark green leaves, or by its scarlet berries enclosed in a sheath. The root is a tuber, roughly spherical, larger than a chestnut, containing an acrid principle that partly disappears on drying, along with mucilage and a great deal of starch.

**Sweet Woodruff.** — The *Stellina odorosa* (sweet woodruff) is an herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family. It is found in the shaded woods of the mountains. Its flowers are very tiny and white.

It has a thin, creeping rhizome, and a square stem 15–25 cm tall. When fresh it has little scent, but when dried it takes on an odor similar to that of tonka bean, due to the coumarin it contains.
