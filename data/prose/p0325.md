# Cognac and Armagnac: Legal Definitions and Production

On this subject, we find the votes of congresses, the opinions of authoritative personalities, and even court judgments from tribunals and appellate courts.

We, instead, shall cite the definition approved by the First International Congress for the Repression of Fraud in Foodstuffs, held in Geneva in 1908, which has been recalled on other occasions and may be considered to have official value.

Cognac, or brandy from the Charente region, is the product of the distillation of natural wines harvested and distilled within the administrative boundaries of Charente and Lower Charente (France), following the so-called Charentais procedures.

The Congress itself, by majority vote, expressed the opinion that brandies obtained by distilling natural wines in general cannot be qualified with this denomination—now established in common usage—and that therefore it is not advisable to continue applying it.

Armagnac, or Armagnac brandy, is the product of the distillation of wines harvested and distilled in the Armagnac region.

Moreover, cognac is distilled throughout central and southern France, in Spain and Portugal, and also in the wine-producing regions of the Rhine and other wine regions of Germany. The fineness of cognac depends on the qualities of the wine that is distilled. It is natural that only those wines that have little value as such are generally distilled. Nevertheless, special wines are produced for this distillation, made rich in alcohol and aroma by adding alcohol and ethers before fermentation. The grape skins are preferably the factors contributing to aromatic bouquet. Poor vintages for wine generally provoke a high yield of cognac.

Cognac too, when it comes out of the still, is colorless, and if placed immediately in bottles, it remains colorless. However, it improves greatly in wooden casks, and every year gains in fineness. There is no other distillate that refines with age as cognac does, which is why its price also increases with every year of age.
