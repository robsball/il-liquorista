# Causes of Clouding (continued)

The weaker the alcohol, the smaller the quantity of essential oil it can dissolve or retain.

Often, for reasons of taste, this principle is disregarded. In such cases the result is evidently a waste of essence, since beyond a certain limit—set by the above principle—the essence no longer contributes to a more pronounced flavor. Beyond this limit, the essential oil separates out without producing any further effect.

The quantities of essential oil that a given alcohol can dissolve are not the same for all oils; indeed, they can vary even for the same essential oil, depending on its age and on the temperature used during its preparation.

Essential oils should therefore be divided into those that are easily soluble and those that are difficult to dissolve. Among the former are those that are dissolved by alcohol in large quantity; among the latter, those dissolved only in small quantity. Among the essential oils that are difficult to dissolve are, among others, those of citron, orange, zedoary, cubeb, and juniper berries.

To dissolve these, the greatest care must be taken.

**2°** Clouding may also be caused by the aging (oxidation/resinification) of the essential oils. Such oils are retained by alcohol only when it is of high strength. When mixed with water, they separate more readily than if they were fresh.

Care should therefore be taken to purchase only the quantity of essential oils actually needed; furthermore, they should be stored in dark glass bottles, well stoppered, protected from light, and kept in cool (not cold!) places.

The yellowing of colorless essential oils and the browning of yellow ones are signs of resinification. These oils are less prone to spoiling if kept mixed with a sufficient quantity of alcohol. While still fresh, they should be mixed with an equal weight of absolute alcohol—understood to be free of fusel oil—or with enough alcohol at 90% to keep the essences in solution. In this way the essential oil retains its good flavor as well as its solubility.

**3°** Clouding can also be caused by the addition of water too quickly and carelessly.
