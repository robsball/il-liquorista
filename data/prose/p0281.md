# Raspberry Juice Preservation and Fermentation Methods

The filtered clear juice is used to fill bottles of one kilogram capacity, which are placed in a water bath (bain-marie), leaving them there until approximately 20 grams of juice have escaped from the bottles. Seal immediately, apply sealing wax to the cork, and store lying down. Juice prepared in this manner remains unaltered for years. For every kilogram of juice, take two kilograms of sugar, bring to a boil once, and strain. To filter rapidly and with little loss of juice, one may use scraps of filter paper that are shredded in water to form a paste, which is placed in a cloth bag; the water is removed by pressing, and the juice to be filtered is poured over the paste.

**Method 2 (W. Noerr):** The fruits are crushed or pressed; the juice is treated with 2% sugar and allowed to ferment in a well-sealed bottle, filled two-thirds full, with a bent glass tube inserted into the cork, its open end extended with a small piece of rubber tubing, immersed 1 cm into a bottle nearly full of water.

Fermentation is normally completed after 10 days, which is evident when, on agitating the container holding the juice, no more bubbles of carbon dioxide develop. To remove proteinaceous substances and the yeast formed, filter by pouring the first portions of liquid repeatedly over the filter until the juice runs clear rapidly. Add the necessary quantity of sugar, allow it to rest for half a day, then bring to a boil once, strain, and immediately pour into warm earthenware bottles. The addition of sugar to the juice before fermentation has the advantage that fermentation proceeds more completely and the juice, due to alcoholic fermentation, assumes a beautiful dark-red color. Fermentation before pressing considerably decreases the aroma or color.
