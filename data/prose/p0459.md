There is no guarantee, given the diversity of products according to their origin, their ease of degradation, and the frequent adulterations to which they are subject, and the corresponding difficulty in detecting them. These adulterations also cause a certain uncertainty in results, since one cannot have a reliable standard for the efficacy of the raw materials employed. Nevertheless, the manufacture of liqueurs by means of essential oils and essences offers such advantages in simplicity over other methods that small-scale industry generally gives it preference, taking those precautions that serve—insofar as possible—to avoid the aforementioned drawback of degradation or adulteration.

As for the results obtained, it is useful to observe that, depending on the case, they may be preferable or less so than those obtained with other methods.

Some contend that liqueurs prepared by maceration are preferable to those obtained by means of essential oils. But, we repeat, one must distinguish.

It is evident that in maceration there dissolve in the alcohol and water employed, not only the essential oils, but also other aromatic and bitter principles, etc. Given this premise, it is no less evident that these dissolved principles may be of such a nature as to modify for better or worse the final product of the work (see p. 126).

It follows from this consideration that it is not possible to establish a priori that the results obtained with maceration must necessarily be better than those obtained with mixtures of essential oils; rather, it is fitting to study which of the two methods merits preference depending on the substances employed and the purpose one wishes to achieve.

And it is precisely here that the utility of this manual becomes apparent, with the aid of which one can avoid failures or fruitless attempts. Those products whose preparation is indicated here by different methods are those in which the soluble principles we mentioned earlier exercise little or no influence on the final result; while the others, whose preparation is indicated in only one way, are those for which this method of preparation yields the best results.
