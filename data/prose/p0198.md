11. Schott recommends passing through the liquor a current of air cooled below 0°C, thereby achieving the oxidation of the higher (fusel) alcohols or the removal of the ethers that form.

It has also been found highly effective — especially for the artificial aging of brandies and cognacs (1) — to apply freezing, at a temperature of 120–130 degrees below zero.
