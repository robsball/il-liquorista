**Elecampane.** — *Inula helenium* (Compositae) is a large and handsome plant, very common in our damp meadows and shady places. The root is used, which is found in commerce in pieces about 10 cm long by 2 to 4 cm [thick], with many long rootlets. It is a hard, tough rhizome, yellowish-brown on the outside, white inside; it has an aromatic odor recalling camphor and orris root; the taste is bitter and aromatic.

**Fennel.** — The fruits, commonly called seeds, of *Anethum foeniculum* (Umbelliferae), are distinguished according to their origin into the following types:

1°. German or Saxon fennel, produced by *Foeniculum vulgare*: it consists of oblong, almost cylindrical grains, slightly curved, 4-8 mm long, easily split into two parts; they are greenish-gray or brownish in color, have a sweet and pungent taste, and a distinctive aromatic odor. The most prized qualities are those from Saxony, followed by those from Thuringia and Galicia.

2°. Roman or sweet fennel, fruits coming from sweet fennel, which is cultivated in France and southern Italy; the fruits are similar to the preceding but larger (8-15 mm), with more prominent ribs, lighter color, and stronger, more aromatic odor and taste.

3°. Indian fennel; shorter fruits; it is less prized.

4°. Wild or bitter fennel; this is the fruit of plants grown spontaneously (wild). It is smaller than the preceding (4-5 mm), has a brownish color, and a somewhat bitter taste.
