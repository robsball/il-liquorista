# Kirsch and Its Imitations

## Evaporation

We should note in passing that little regard is paid to Kirsch that is colored to any degree whatsoever; rather, the clearer it is, the more highly it is prized.

The Kirsch found in commerce is sometimes the result of distillation of poor-quality spirit in which peach leaves or cherry laurel leaves have been steeped. These liquors are of inferior quality and can even cause serious harm due to the dissolution of toxic principles contained in peach and cherry laurel leaves.

## Kirsch Cut or Half Kirsch

To a mixture of 12 liters of alcohol at 85° and 1.8 liters of water, add a few drops of ammonia, then dissolve 70–80 g of white sugar and add:

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Distilled water of cherry laurel | 250 cc |
| Distilled water of orange blossom | 50 cc |
| Brandy or cognac | 200 cc |

Filter the mixture through paper twice over 50 g of well-washed, dry vegetable charcoal, and finally add 1 liter of genuine, pure Kirsch to the filtered liquid.

## Imitations

With regard to artificial products—as we repeat—they turn out to be far inferior to the natural product; nevertheless, various kinds are found in commerce, beginning with common well-purified wine spirit, to which a few drops of essential oil of bitter almonds or cherry laurel are added, up to more complex mixtures, of which we shall indicate some recipes.

One can also employ the following extracts or essences by adding them to the finest spirit:

### Formula I

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Essential oil of rose | 2 drops |
| Water of bitter almonds | 30 g |
| Alcohol at 95° | 4 kg |
| Syrup of cherries | 4 kg |
| Distilled water | 5 kg |
