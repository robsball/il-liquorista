# Cherry Liqueur Production (continued)

## Fermentation and Distillation Process

The time required for this fermentation varies from 15 to 30 days. Until fermentation is complete, care must be taken daily to stir and to soak the upper layer of cherries, so as not to leave any part of the mass exposed to air contact for too long. Without this precaution, the surface will turn to vinegar; neglecting it risks the loss of the entire contents of the barrel. An indispensable precaution to take, when fermentation is nearing its end, is not to wait for it to be entirely complete before beginning distillation. Otherwise, the mass of cherries, which has been lifted by gas development, will fall to the bottom of the barrel. This causes a mixture of the principles deposited at the lower part with the upper layer of the mass, and the result yields neither good quality nor satisfactory quantity.

## Method II

Collect the cherries with care when ripe, removing the stems and setting aside any damaged fruit. Once a sufficiently large quantity has been gathered to begin operations, spread them on a somewhat concave wicker basket placed over a barrel; the juice drips through. Weigh the remaining solids and crush only one-quarter of them; pour everything into the barrel, cover it, and allow to ferment. Once fermentation is complete, strain the clear liquid, transfer it to an alembic, and distill. In this manner one obtains a liqueur of excellent flavor.

## Method III

Crush the cherries and separate out the whole pits. Macerate the pulp with alcohol for two days; then strain the clear liquid and add parts equal to quantities of cherry laurel essential oil. Once well dissolved, add the water, filter, and store.
