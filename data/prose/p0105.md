# Quassia — Rhubarb

...resembles roughly in shape a lung. It has a greenish-gray color. It is used to give bitterness to beer in place of hops.

**Quassia.** — Quassia wood, bitter wood. This is the wood of *Quassia amara*, a shrub that grows in Surinam, Guiana, Colombia, etc. This wood is found in commerce in very irregular cylindrical pieces, up to a meter long, and 5 to 10 cm in diameter.

It is light and tough; the bark is pale gray, thin, spotted with blackish lichens, and detaches easily; the wood is pale yellow in color, odorless, with an intense and very persistent bitter taste; this taste is readily communicated to water in which the wood is left to macerate; it is due to quassin. Related to the above is *Quassia excelsa* (Jamaica quassia). Its wood is found in commerce in thick pieces (3 decimeters), longer and heavier, with bark that adheres more firmly; the wood is whitish, mottled with yellowish-green.

**Rhubarb.** — The most prized comes from China, from Canton and the Indies, and is supplied by the root of *Rheum officinale* (Polygonaceae), in pieces of varying shape, 8 to 10 cm long by 5 to 8 cm thick. Chinese rhubarb is covered with a yellow powder, which sometimes retains traces of the remains of the brown bark; once freed of the powder, it presents a somewhat rough, orange-yellow surface, streaked with a fine network of oval or rhombic mesh, whitish in color. It has a strong odor and an astringent bitter taste; when chewed it stains the saliva yellow; the powder is a bright yellow color.

Several qualities of Chinese rhubarb are known, which can easily be distinguished from one another by taste and smell.

From Asia there formerly came Muscovy rhubarb, also called Russian or Tartary rhubarb, which was highly prized. It is no longer found in commerce today.

In Europe the rhubarb plant is cultivated, but the products obtained are much inferior to the Chinese ones. The best European rhubarb is the English variety, obtained from *Rheum palmatum*; it often closely resembles the Chinese type. In Austria-Hungary, rhapontic rhubarb is cultivated, which is of poor quality.
