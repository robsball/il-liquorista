Finally, nowadays a special class may be considered to be that of ready-made Extracts or Essences, which are not used as they are but are diluted, either to prepare beverages (Punch Extracts, etc.), in which case they must contain a high percentage of sugar; or to prepare liqueurs proper, in which case they must consist of alcoholic tinctures of spices or of essential oils, highly concentrated in aromatic principles, without sugar. — To these Extracts for liqueurs we shall devote a special chapter.

---

Another, simpler classification, adopted in France, is based on the quality of the liqueurs, according to the proportions of alcohol and sugar they contain:

| Category | Alcohol content (85°–90°) | Sugar | Water |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1) Ordinary liqueurs | 25% by volume in liters (including the quantity of spirits or tinctures used in the composition) | 12–15 kg | remainder, to complete 100 liters |
| 2) Semi-fine liqueurs | 28–30% by volume in liters | 25 kg | remainder, to complete 100 liters |
| 3) Fine liqueurs | 32–35% by volume in liters | 36–45 kg | distilled water, remainder, to complete 100 liters |
| 4) Superfine liqueurs (also called French liqueurs, Island liqueurs, etc.), prepared with great care and with the finest materials | 36–60 liters | white sugar 48–56 kg | distilled water, remainder, to complete 100 liters |
