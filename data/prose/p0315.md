# On Blending, Imitation, and Quality of Distilled Spirits

Aromatic and coloring substances are employed in the same form as provided by the distillation apparatus, and according to their age—gaining in quality—they assume the designation of pure or genuine.

However, it is not always possible to bring all these distillates to market in their original form. In Rhum, for example, the intense penetrating aroma would be an obstacle.

In most cases, however, the obstacle is represented by the elevated price of these liquors, which would make them inaccessible to the great majority of consumers.

One is therefore compelled to reduce their price by blending them with less expensive liquors and with substances suited to impart aroma to them.

This procedure is called *blending*, if to prepare a product of lower price one uses a quantity still fairly considerable of the genuine liquor. In some cases, however, even with these blends one cannot satisfy consumer demand, and the manufacturer is then forced to resort to imitations, which have in common with true distillates only the name.

Such imitations, even if well prepared, cannot be compared—neither in flavor, nor in aroma, nor in properties—to true or blended liquors.

They are generally ordinary spirits, neither better nor worse than these, and since they are equally harmless and moreover indispensable to that class of consumers who wish to taste in the spirit scarcely anything but alcohol flavor without paying the fair price, one cannot but justify in some manner these imitations so long as they are not placed on the market as genuine liquors.

For rhum, arrack, and cognac, but especially for rhum, the consumption of blends is far greater than that of genuine distillates.

Some manufacturers believe that blends made cold approach genuine distillates closely. This is an error. True distillates cannot be prepared by any artificial means.

The whole secret lies in blending genuine distillates with the finest alcohol; every other addition damages quality. Since one cannot satisfy consumers regarding price, it is prudent to adhere to aromatics and good tinctures. These are the additions that prove least harmful.
