# Vermouth: Selection and Preparation of the Base Wine

The name derives from the German designation for wormwood (*Wermouth*), which constitutes not always the fundamental ingredient, but certainly one of the most important components entering into the composition of the mixture of herbs and plants with which wine is aromatized and rendered bitter and agreeable in vermouth.

The wine to be employed for the preparation, or rather, the transformation into vermouth must be of excellent quality, of regular acidity and not overly rich in aroma. For this purpose, Moscato wines from Monferrato work very well, as do the white wines from Astigiano and Canelli, as well as the Campidano type, wines from Ischia, Sardinia, Sicily, Romagna, and finally also the so-called Samos type.

Moreover, this wine should preferably be aged; young wines and those of ordinary quality will never produce a fine vermouth, even if the subsequent treatment to aromatize it is careful.

Generally white wine is employed; however, some producers have also adopted red wine, or a mixture of white and red wine. We should add that vermouth can also be prepared without wine, using simply a mixture of water and alcohol of the stated strength, and suitably sweetened.

Always prefer a dry Moscato wine of two years of age, better if three: in its absence, an old, dry white wine serves, to which, if possible, it would be well to add 30% Moscato. The essential requirement for the successful production of vermouth is that the white wine be sound, of frank flavor, and perfectly clear. This wine should be alcoholized so that it has 15–17% alcohol if the vermouth is to travel; if it is instead to be consumed locally, alcoholization is not necessary provided the wine naturally has 12–14% alcohol.

The alcoholic strength of vermouth generally oscillates between 14° and 17° by volume; and is therefore achieved by addition of alcohol of good flavor, or previously aromatized with the herbs, to the chosen wine, of which it is therefore necessary to always know or evaluate the alcoholic strength.
