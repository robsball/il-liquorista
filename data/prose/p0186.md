### Observations

1. To facilitate the dissolving of sugar in heavily sugared liqueurs and to prevent it from crystallizing, 1 liter of glucose is added per hectoliter of liqueur, naturally taking this addition into account when calculating the amount of sugar to be used.

2. Part of the sugar can be replaced with purified glycerin (about 1/10 of the weight of the sugar). With this addition, the liqueurs keep better. We also note that glycerin is an excellent solvent for aromatic and coloring substances (saffron, indigo, cochineal, etc.); for this reason its addition can be beneficial both to the coloring of the liqueur and to its aroma, allowing suitable substances to be added, which is not always possible with other solvents.

### Filtration

Since one of the main requirements of a liqueur is complete clarity, it is necessary always to carry out a perfect filtration, even when the ingredients used do not contain impurities, as is the case, for example, with preparations made using essential oils.

Filtration is obviously necessary in preparations made by maceration or with fruit juices, although with resting and decanting good results can sometimes be obtained.

However, since the prolonged resting period necessary to achieve perfect clarity is not always compatible with the good qualities of the liqueur — which, indeed, it often causes to deteriorate or at least weaken, due to the volatilization of essential oils or to secondary fermentation, etc. — resorting to filtration sometimes becomes a necessity.
