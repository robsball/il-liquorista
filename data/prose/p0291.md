## Preservation of Purified Juices

To prevent the alteration of juices, or to deprive them of contact with air, the Appert process is advisable, which is without any inconvenience and gives good results, superior to any other method; it imparts no foreign flavor or odor, and preserves the juices perfectly.

We shall recall it in its practical application. Having selected good-quality corks of fine texture and solid bottles, each bottle is filled with juice up to the base of the neck; they are closed exactly by forcibly inserting suitable corks, which are secured by means of a wire adapted firmly to the neck and crisscrossed.

The bottles thus filled and prepared are wrapped in a strong canvas sack: they are placed one after another in a flat-bottomed cauldron: water is introduced into this cauldron in such quantity that the bottles emerge only at the neck, and this water is gradually heated until it comes to a boil. After a few moments of boiling, the fire is extinguished, and the whole is left to rest until the water has become lukewarm.

Then the bottles are examined, those that have broken by chance are separated, and those that have resisted are sealed with a lute made of lime and cheese. Finally they are placed in the cellar spread out on sand. It should be noted that following these operations the juices sometimes become turbid or precipitate some time later, leaving sediment on the bottom.
