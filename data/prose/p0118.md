# Purification and Preservation of Essential Oils

However, in order to ensure the best preservation of essential oils, especially those intended for liqueur-making, it is advisable to mix them, as soon as they are received from the supplier, with very pure alcohol, either in a proportion of 50% or of 10%. Such a mixture, besides preventing easy spoilage, has the advantage of providing a very valuable material for the successful production of the liqueur, because through the prolonged contact between the alcohol and the essential oil, the latter acts upon the alcohol in such a way that the aroma becomes more intimately blended, improved, and more intense. In any case, it is advisable that the essential oils or concentrated essences to be used in preparing a liqueur be diluted in alcohol at least one month before the final mixing of the ingredients for the composition of the liqueur.

It should be understood that in the recipes given below, when the quantity of essential oil to be used as pure essence is indicated, it must instead be multiplied by 2 or by 10, depending on whether the 50% or 10% dilution in alcohol has been used, and consequently the weight of the alcohol indicated as an ingredient should be reduced by the amount already added and mixed with the essences.

We should note, finally, that alcohol alone does not always succeed in dissolving essential oils: for some — juniper, orange, lemon — the addition of a little magnesium carbonate or calcined magnesia is also required. The paste is thinned and poured into a volume of alcohol at 90° ten times greater; the mixture is shaken until the essential oil is completely dissolved. For others, instead — fennel, anise — it is advisable to use powdered sugar: the paste is thinned as described above and then filtered.

Essential oils and essences, before being used in the liqueur mixture, must be filtered: a covered funnel is used, of the kind described further on in the section on Manipulations.
