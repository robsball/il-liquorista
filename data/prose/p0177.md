# Operation of the Extraction Apparatus

...at will, it serves for the distillation and recovery of the solvent when this is alcoholic, ethereal, etc.

The apparatus is set into operation as follows:

The inner vessel *g*, fitted with a woolen cloth serving as a filter and tied around its perforated bottom, is filled to about two-thirds with the material from which the substance is to be extracted, and (see Fig. 11) is suspended in the middle of the extractor *a*. Enough solvent is then poured into the extractor until the space between the bottom of the extractor and that of vessel *g* is filled halfway, taking care at the same time to soak the material in vessel *g* with the same solvent until it is saturated.

After hermetically fitting the two parts *a* and *b* of the apparatus one on top of the other, the lower part is heated by means of a water bath, or directly over the fire if the solvent is water, while at the same time the upper part is suitably cooled. The vapors then rise through the tubes of the condenser, condense there, and fall back as liquid onto the material, exhausting it as they filter through the cloth.
