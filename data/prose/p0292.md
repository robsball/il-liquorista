# Extraction of Citrus and Berry Juices

## Processing Method

Reduce to pulp using a grater, taking care not to touch any membranous capsule at the center, which contains seeds rich in mucilage. Subject the pulp to pressure using a press or squeeze it forcefully, collecting the juice in a glass or earthenware vessel and allow it to ferment. When completely clear, filter through paper after decantation.

## Citron (Cedro)

Remove the rind and white inner layer, crush the fruit by hand while removing the seeds, arrange the pulp in a cloth in layers with wood shavings (previously washed), then press and strain the juice through linen cloth. Allow it to rest in earthenware or glass vessels for four or five days until well purified and the slight fermentation that had been established has ceased. Decant then and filter through unwaxed paper.

Citron seeds contain a principle of great bitterness that communicates to the juice if allowed to remain in it. Nevertheless, when operating on a larger scale, seed extraction becomes quite difficult. In such cases it is preferable to crush the citrons quickly or press them before the juice has had time to act on the seeds, as it would become very difficult to clarify. An essential condition for obtaining good citron juice is therefore to act with speed, and contrary to the precept of Baumé, who recommends leaving the crushed fruits in maceration for 24 hours before distilling them.

The juice of Oranges or Mandarins is prepared in the same manner.

## Currants (Ribes)

Press the cleaned currants by hand through a horsehair sieve, receiving the juice in an earthenware vessel. Press the residue firmly and abandon it in a cool cellar for some time, until as a consequence of the fermentation that becomes established, it offers a clear liquid portion well separated from the gelatinous coagulum. Then pour everything onto a cloth filter.
