# Observations on the Preceding Recipes

## Observations on the Preceding Recipes

In general, when alcohol is indicated in a recipe, it is advisable to first soak the mixture of aromatic substances with it, and then pour the wine over this; or alternatively to prepare the extract by displacement.

**VI.** — The substances are left to macerate in 5 liters of 90° alcohol; to the filtered tincture are added 50 liters of sweet white wine, 85 liters of water, and 15 kg of sugar. Color with 100 grams of caramel and clarify with gelatin. To obtain a finer quality, instead of water and wine, use only the latter (135 liters), treating the ingredients with boiling water and adding only 1.5 kg of sugar.

**XIV.** — The various substances are chopped and crushed, then left to macerate in wine; the liquid is then separated and sugar and alcohol are added in such quantities as judgment and taste dictate, and according to the more or less perfect quality of the wine. Once the sugar is dissolved, filter with fish glue and egg white powder; preserve in clean, odor-free containers.

**XV.** — The aromatics are left to macerate in a barrel of excellent sweet white wine for a period of 24 hours; then taste it, and if it seems insufficiently fragrant, leave the aforementioned aromatics in maceration for a few more hours. Then strain the liquid and bottle it. The leftover aromatics can serve for another 12 to 15 liters of wine.
